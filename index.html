RFarrington MethodQuasi-Poisson / NB GLM

Public Health Surveillance: UK Salmonella Outbreak Early Warning

I analysed 312 weeks of Salmonella Agona case data to build an early-warning system that could flag outbreaks before they escalate. After testing three different statistical models, the Negative Binomial GLM turned out to be the most accurate, catching more genuine outbreaks with fewer false alarms than either Quasi-Poisson approach. What really stood out was that the improved model picked up the Year 2 outbreak 2 weeks sooner than the basic one, which in a real NHS or UKHSA setting could mean faster public health interventions and fewer people getting sick.

UK Animal Welfare: Data-Driven Rehoming Optimisation

Working with a UK animal charity, I looked into whether certain dog breeds take longer to rehome than others, and whether any of them were meeting the national 27-week target. The numbers showed that Greyhounds get rehomed significantly quicker than Dobermanns, which gave the charity a clear reason to shift their foster placement efforts and advertising spend towards the breeds that need more help. I validated everything with Kolmogorov-Smirnov and Shapiro-Wilk tests, and put together a 95% confidence interval report that the team used to plan their 2025 fostering strategy.
